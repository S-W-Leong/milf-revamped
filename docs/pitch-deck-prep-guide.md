# TheFirst Spark Challenge — Pitch Deck Preparation Guide

> A self-serve guide to what your pitch deck needs to include — so judges understand who you're helping, what you're solving, and why it matters.

---

## The Common Pitch Mistake

| | |
|---|---|
| **⚠ WEAK PITCH** | "We built an AI app that helps with healthcare." |
| **✓ STRONGER PITCH** | "We help caregivers understand discharge instructions after a hospital visit, so they can manage medication, follow-ups, and warning signs more confidently." |

The stronger pitch names a specific user, a specific moment, and a specific outcome. The weak pitch names a technology and a vertical.

---

## Pitch Spine

Use this framework to fill in the building blocks of your pitch before writing a single slide.

| Component | Prompt |
|---|---|
| **Challenge Statement** | We are responding to: ___ |
| **Target User** | Our first target user is: ___ |
| **Specific Use Case** | The moment where this problem happens is: ___ |
| **Pain Point** | The user struggles with: ___ |
| **Core Bottleneck** | This problem happens because: ___ |
| **Current Alternative** | Today, they usually solve it by: ___ |
| **Value Proposition** | Our solution helps them: ___ |
| **How It Works** | It works by: ___ |

### One-Line Output Formula

> We help **[user]** who struggle with **[pain]** during **[use case]** by solving **[bottleneck]** through **[solution]** so they can **[valuable outcome]**.

---

## Recommended Pitch Deck Flow

Build your deck in this order. Each slide answers one question.

| # | Slide | Question to Answer |
|---|---|---|
| 01 | **Challenge Statement** | What challenge are you responding to? |
| 02 | **Target User & Use Case** | Who exactly faces this problem, and when? |
| 03 | **Pain Point & Bottleneck** | What is hard, broken, slow, or confusing today? |
| 04 | **Current Alternative** | What do users currently do instead? |
| 05 | **Solution & Value Proposition** | What do you provide, and what outcome improves? |
| 06 | **How It Works** | Show the product flow, workflow, or demo. |
| 07 | **Traction & Validation** *(bonus)* | Any evidence, user feedback, or data that strengthens your pitch. |

## Don't Just Name a Target Audience

Specificity is what separates a forgettable pitch from a memorable one.

| Level | Example |
|---|---|
| **Too Broad** | Young women |
| **Better** | Women in their 20s and early 30s navigating career growth and long-term family planning |
| **Best** | A 27–32-year-old early-to-mid career woman in a high-growth role making major career decisions — promotion, overseas posting, startup opportunity, MBA, or job switch — while quietly wondering how fertility, finances, relationships, and timing may affect her future options. |

## The ICP Clarity Ladder

Work your way up to Level 4. That is your target ICP.

| Level | Definition | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Level 1** | Industry / sector only | "Healthcare workers" |
| **Level 2** | Role + context | "Nurses in public hospitals" |
| **Level 3** | Role + moment | "Nurses during patient discharge" |
| **Level 4 ✓** | Role + moment + struggle | "Nurses managing high-volume discharge who struggle to confirm patients understood instructions" |

## Pain Point vs. Bottleneck

These are two distinct things. Your solution must attack the bottleneck — not just acknowledge the pain.

### Pain Point
**What it is:** What the user struggles with — the symptom they feel.

**Healthcare example:** Caregivers often leave the hospital confused about medication instructions, follow-up appointments, and warning signs to watch for.

### Bottleneck
**What it is:** What *stops* them from solving it today — the root cause your solution must attack.

**Healthcare example:** Information is given verbally or across multiple documents — caregivers may not know what questions to ask before discharge.

> **Your solution must attack the bottleneck — not just acknowledge the pain.**

---

## Judge Readiness Checklist

Before you pitch, verify a judge could answer all five of these from your deck alone.

- [ ] Who is this for?
- [ ] What real problem are they facing?
- [ ] What is the bottleneck?
- [ ] How does your solution remove that bottleneck?
- [ ] Why does this meaningfully address the challenge?

**If a judge can answer all five, your pitch is judge-ready.**
